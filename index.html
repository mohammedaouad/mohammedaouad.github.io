AutoRegCT
Automatic pre/post-op CT registration for THA
+
SimpleITKTotalSegmentator StreamlitSciPypytest

Automates the registration workflow that took 25 to 30 minutes per patient during my bachelor thesis. The pipeline segments the femur from both scans automatically, rigidly registers the pre-op into the post-op space, and computes 7 implant positioning metrics.

Mattes mutual information with a 3-level multi-resolution pyramid was chosen for its robustness to metal streak artifacts in post-op CT. Validated on 4 synthetic test cases including a metal artifact simulation, where registration recovered 7.56 degrees of a known 8 degree rotation.

Interface
CLI + Streamlit GUI
Metric
Mattes MI, 3-level pyramid
Outputs
7 positioning metrics

StemVisualization
3D surgical planning tool for total hip arthroplasty
+
PyQt6PyVistaSimpleITK TotalSegmentatorSciPyNumPy

Replaces manual landmark placement in 3D Slicer with a fully automatic geometric pipeline. Given a femur STL, it fits a sphere to the femoral head for HJC detection, computes the shaft axis via SVD, places the stem tip by CCD angle scanning, and applies a signed-distance neck resection. The full pipeline runs in under 2 seconds on CPU.

A DICOM-to-STL preprocessing script using SimpleITK and TotalSegmentator enables a fully automated path from raw CT data to interactive surgical planning without any manual segmentation.

Interface
Dual-viewport Qt GUI
Key method
PCA + SVD + sphere fitting
Input
DICOM or STL

Brain Tumor Segmentation
U-Net for binary tumor segmentation on BraTS 2020
+
PyTorchU-NetGrad-CAM h5pyNumPyMatplotlib

Full segmentation pipeline built from scratch on BraTS 2020, covering data loading, U-Net architecture, Dice loss, training loop, inference, and Grad-CAM visualization. Trained on 57,195 axial slices across 369 patients on a T4 GPU in Google Colab.

The model reached a Dice loss of 0.2524 after 5 epochs and achieved a Dice score of 0.937 on the validation sample. Grad-CAM is implemented with PyTorch hooks on the bottleneck layer and produces a spatial heatmap showing which input regions drove each prediction.

04
Blood Pump PID Control
System identification and PID controller for an implantable centrifugal pump
+
SciPyNumPyMatplotlib Control systemsZiegler-Nichols

Models a three-block implantable centrifugal pump (MDU, motor/pump, flow sensor), fits PT1 and PT2 transfer functions to step response data using scipy curve fitting, and designs a PID controller to keep flow stable between 2 and 6 L/min.

Auto-tuning uses Ziegler-Nichols via phase crossover from the Bode response. The raw Z-N gain is de-rated by 35% to reduce overshoot. Step response settles in around 30 seconds with less than 5% overshoot. An interactive matplotlib dashboard shows the closed-loop simulation live.
