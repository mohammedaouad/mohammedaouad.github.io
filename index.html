AutoRegCT

Automatic pre/post-op CT registration for total hip arthroplasty
Python SimpleITK TotalSegmentator Streamlit pytest

Research prototype for automatic comparison of pre-operative and post-operative CT data in THA. The pipeline combines femur segmentation, rigid registration, overlay visualization, and quantitative registration metrics.

The goal is to reduce manual alignment and measurement work and make the evaluation more reproducible. Synthetic test cases are used to validate whether known rotations and translations can be recovered.

StemVisualization

3D surgical planning prototype for femoral stem placement
PyQt6 PyVista SimpleITK SciPy NumPy

Prototype tool for visualizing femur geometry and stem placement in total hip arthroplasty. It estimates the hip joint center, computes the shaft axis, places a stem line, and supports CCD-angle and neck-offset exploration.

The project connects raw CT-derived geometry with interactive planning views, making it useful for exploring implant positioning and leg length discrepancy effects.

Blood Pump PID Control

System identification and PID control for a centrifugal blood pump model
SciPy NumPy Matplotlib Control Systems Ziegler-Nichols

The project connects medical device context with classical control engineering: model fitting, controller tuning, overshoot reduction, and closed-loop response analysis.

About

Software for medical imaging, built from clinical context.

I am an MSc Medical Engineering student at FH Aachen, focused on medical imaging software, CT registration, and surgical planning tools.

My background started in the clinic as a cardio technician, where I worked around pre-operative checks and intraoperative monitoring. That experience still influences how I approach software: the result has to be understandable, reliable, and useful in a real workflow.

My bachelor thesis at the Helmholtz Institute compared 3D Slicer and ImFusion for CT segmentation in total hip arthroplasty using data from 201 patients. Since October 2024, I have continued at Helmholtz through my master’s practical project, working on femur geometry, implant positioning, and leg length discrepancy.

I build MATLAB and Python prototypes for segmentation, registration, 3D visualization, and surgical planning. My main interest is software that reduces manual work in clinical image analysis and makes measurements more reproducible.

In parallel, I work as a HiWi on the Healthy Living Space project, where I develop and test Beckhoff TwinCAT 3 control logic for ventilation, heating, humidity, and sensor-based room systems. This gives me practical experience with automation, debugging, and engineering systems that need to run reliably.
